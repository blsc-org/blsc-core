## Our Pledge
As contributors, maintainers, and members of the BLSC community, we pledge to create an open, accessible, and respectful environment for everyone.  
We are committed to contributing to a project that advances Bulgarian language technology, digital heritage, and scientific research in a constructive and collaborative manner.

We pledge to make participation in our project a harassment-free experience for all, regardless of:
- technical background  
- academic level  
- geographic location  
- cultural, linguistic, or social background  

## Our Standards

### Examples of Behavior That Contributes to a Positive Environment:
- Demonstrating empathy, respect, and kindness toward others  
- Being open to constructive feedback  
- Focusing on project-related issues and technical improvements  
- Helping new contributors understand the project structure and standards  
- Acknowledging the work of others and valuing collaboration  

### Examples of Unacceptable Behavior:
- Insults, personal attacks, trolling, or demeaning comments  
- Harassment or intimidation  
- Publishing private information without permission  
- Disruptive or intentionally unproductive behavior  
- Knowingly contributing illegal or copyrighted material  
- Attempting to override project governance or bypass review processes  

## Responsibilities of Maintainers
Project maintainers are responsible for:
- Defining and enforcing the standards of acceptable behavior  
- Reviewing contributions in a fair and transparent manner  
- Taking appropriate corrective action when unacceptable behavior occurs  
- Protecting the integrity and legality of the project  
- Ensuring consistent application of contribution guidelines  

Maintainers have the right to:
- Edit, reject, or request changes to contributions  
- Temporarily or permanently ban contributors for behavior that violates this Code of Conduct  
- Close or lock discussions that violate community standards  

## Scope
This Code of Conduct applies:
- within project repositories  
- in issues, pull requests, discussions, and comments  
- in community spaces, whether public or private  
- when representing the project or community in any context

## Enforcement Guidelines

### 1. Correction  
**Consequence:** Private or public reminder about project standards.

### 2. Warning  
**Consequence:** Formal warning with a clear description of the violation and expected behavior.

### 3. Temporary Suspension  
**Consequence:** Contributor is temporarily barred from interacting with the community.

### 4. Permanent Ban  
**Consequence:** Permanent removal from all project spaces for severe or repeated violations.
